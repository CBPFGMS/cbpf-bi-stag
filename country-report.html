Lessons Learned

Effective Coordination and Partnerships

Close collaboration between the Humanitarian Coordinator, UN agencies, and NGOs ensured that CBPF resources were allocated strategically to address priority humanitarian needs while strengthening the humanitarian coordination architecture.

Localization and Capacity Building

Strong emphasis on supporting national and local NGOs through CBPF funding has contributed to building sustainable humanitarian capacity at the country level, enabling more effective and contextualized response to affected communities.

Flexibility in Response

The emergency reserve mechanism demonstrated its critical value in enabling rapid response to sudden-onset crises and emerging needs, often reaching affected populations within days of approval.

Multi-Sectoral Approach

Allocating CBPF resources across multiple sectors (health, food security, protection, shelter, WASH, education) ensured a comprehensive humanitarian response that addressed the diverse needs of affected populations.
